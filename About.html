About The
Danielle Media

Dannielle Media is a purpose-driven creative company built at the intersection of culture, storytelling, impact and they are the official publishers of GBEDU Magazine.

Founded on the belief that media should do more than entertain, Dannielle Media exists to document truth, preserve identity, and shape narratives that matter. With a strong focus on Africa particularly the Yoruba culture, the company uses film, print, digital content, and creative direction as tools to tell stories that are authentic, dignified, and rooted in lived experience.
Every project under Dannielle Media carries intention: to inform, inspire, and leave cultural footprints that will outlive trends.

As the publishing home of GBEDU Magazine, Dannielle Media provides the vision, structure, and creative backbone that allows the magazine to thrive as a cultural archive and a modern platform. The company understands that culture must be presented in ways that resonate with today’s youth while remaining faithful to its origins. This balance of tradition and innovation defines Dannielle Media’s approach to publishing, bold visuals, thoughtful storytelling, and narratives that speak across generations and borders, especially to Africans in the diaspora seeking reconnection. Beyond creativity, Dannielle Media is grounded in values of service and social responsibility. The company aligns its operations with humanitarian goals, supporting causes that uplift people, empower creatives, and respond to real needs within communities. Through GBEDU Magazine and other projects, Dannielle Media channels visibility into impact ensuring that storytelling translates into tangible good. At its core, Dannielle Media is not just a media company; it is a cultural institution in the making. One committed to owning African stories, protecting cultural memory, amplifying overlooked voices, and using media as a force for preservation, pride, and humanity.
