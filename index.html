Gbẹ̀du

GBEDU is more than a magazine; it is a cultural bridge. Born from the deep rhythms, stories, and philosophies of our people. GBEDU exists to document, celebrate, and pass on African, especially Yoruba culture in ways that speak to today’s youths and tomorrow’s elders.

In an age of fast content and fading roots, culture is at risk of becoming aesthetic without meaning. GBEDU steps in as an archive and a compass, preserving language, music, fashion, film, folklore, food, belief systems, and lived experiences, while translating them into formats young people actually engage with. From in-depth features and interviews to visuals and narratives that feel current, GBEDU makes culture accessible without diluting its soul.

For youths in the diaspora, the need is even more urgent. Distance often creates gaps between generations, between language and identity, between pride and understanding. GBEDU becomes an entry point back home. It answers the questions many are quietly asking: Where do I come from? What do these customs mean? How do I belong without pretending? By telling our stories with honesty and style, the magazine reconnects diaspora youths to their roots, not through nostalgia alone, but through relevance.

Locally, GBEDU serves as validation and inspiration. It amplifies voices that have carried culture forward, artists, filmmakers, elders, creatives, thinkers, while spotlighting the next generation shaping culture in new ways. It shows young Nigerians that their heritage is not a limitation, but a creative advantage.

Ultimately, GBEDU is a cultural responsibility. It preserves memory, sparks curiosity, and builds pride. It reminds us that culture is not static, it evolves when it is loved, documented, and shared. And if the future is watching, GBEDU is making sure it knows where it comes from.

Meet Our Creative Director

Akinkunmi LASISI stands at the heart of GBEDU Magazine as its Creative Director and Editor-in-Chief, driven by a clear purpose: to preserve, celebrate, and project Yoruba culture with pride, depth, and modern relevance.
Deeply rooted in Yoruba heritage, Akinkunmi’s love for the culture goes beyond admiration, it is a lifelong commitment. Through storytelling, visuals, language, film, and media, he has consistently used his platforms to spotlight the beauty, values, and resilience of Yoruba traditions. Whether amplifying indigenous narratives, honoring cultural icons, or reintroducing forgotten wisdom in contemporary form, his work reflects a belief that culture must be lived, documented, and shared especially with younger generations at home and in the diaspora.
As a creative, actor, and content creator, Akinkunmi has always seen media as a powerful cultural tool. Under his leadership, GBEDU Magazine is not just a publication but a cultural archive and a living conversation, one that bridges generations, corrects misrepresentations, and reminds youths that their identity is a source of strength, not something to be diluted or hidden.
Beyond culture and creativity, Akinkunmi’s journey has been marked by compassion and service. Over the years, he has quietly and openly engaged in humanitarian efforts, supporting individuals in need, assisting struggling creatives, and extending help during difficult times. These acts are not performative but purposeful, guided by the Yoruba values of omoluabi, community, and responsibility to one another.
True to this spirit, Akinkunmi has committed that all proceeds from GBEDU Magazine will be channeled into humanitarian services. For him, culture without humanity is incomplete. GBEDU is therefore both a celebration and a mission, using storytelling to uplift identity, and using its success to directly impact lives.
In Akinkunmi LASISI, culture finds a devoted custodian, creativity finds direction, and service finds sincerity. Through GBEDU Magazine, he is not only telling our stories, he is ensuring they serve the people.
